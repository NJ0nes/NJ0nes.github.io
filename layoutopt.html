In this project for MAT 552, I designed a method to find a layout of locations that minimizes the total distance traveled for a given route across them with repeat visits in 1 dimension. The problem was originally envisioned as a method for profile-guided optimization in a compiler, the idea being that functions or data could be automatically placed in memory to maximize cache locality for a known access pattern. However, I was also interested in the problem as a sort of inversion of the traveling salesman problem. In the TSP, the locations are fixed and the goal is to find the route; in my problem, the route is fixed and the goal is to find the locations.
